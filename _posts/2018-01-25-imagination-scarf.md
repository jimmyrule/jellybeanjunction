A few months ago the lovely people at Sirdar kindly sent me some of their gorgeous Imagination yarn to try out. It is a soft, chunky weight yarn made from 100% acrylic and comes in 8 different colour ways. I had 4 balls of ‘Moonriver’ which is a mixture of blues, pinks, purples and greens, as we are still in the depths of winter here in the UK I decided to make myself a new scarf – I knew that it would be incredibly soft and cosy to wear.

<img class="rounded img-fluid post-img mx-auto d-block" src="/assets/images/IMG_3150-1-768x768.jpeg">

I am thrilled with the finished scarf which is made using a simple v-stitch, I have included the pattern below. I would definitely recommend this yarn, the photos do not do it’s pretty colour justice and I absolutely loved its soft texture – a pleasure to work with!

**Imagination Scarf Pattern**

What you’ll need:

- 3 balls of Sirdar Imagination Chunky
- 7mm crochet hook
- scissors
- darning needle

Abbreviations:

- ch – Chain
- st – Stitch
- tr – Treble Crochet (US Double Crochet)

Pattern is written in UK terms.

——————–

Chain 37

Row 1 – tr into 5th chain from the hook, ch1, tr into same stitch, \*skip 2 ch, (tr, ch1, tr) in next st, repeat from \* across until you have 2 ch left, tr in last ch, turn.

Row 2 – ch3, \*(tr, ch1, tr) in ch1 space, repeat from \* across to end, tr in top of ch3, turn.

Row 3 to 80 – Repeat Row 2.

Fasten off leaving a long tail for sewing together. Using a darning needle sew the short ends of the scarf together and then weave in the ends to finish.
