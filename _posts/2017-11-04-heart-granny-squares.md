A while ago I was playing around with granny square variations and I made these pretty heart squares. I made 9 in total and they became the front of a cushion cover that I sold in [my Etsy shop](https://www.etsy.com/uk/shop/JellybeanJunctionUK).

<img class="rounded img-fluid post-img mx-auto d-block" src="/assets/images/Snapseed_1.jpeg">

The design is simple but effective and can be made using any yarn in any colours. I used aran weight cotton yarn to make these and decided on a two-tone heart with white surround.

I have written up the pattern for you to make your own…

**Heart Granny Square Pattern**

What you’ll need:

- 3 colours of aran weight cotton yarn
- 4mm crochet hook
- scissors
- darning needle

<img class="rounded img-fluid post-img mx-auto d-block" src="/assets/images/IMG_0625-768x768.jpeg">

Abbreviations:

- ch – Chain
- st – Stitch
- tr – Treble Crochet (US Double Crochet)

Pattern is written in UK terms. Beginning chain **always** counts as first stitch (unless otherwise stated). You are making a basic granny square but changing colours as you go to create the heart design.

——————–

Using central colour (***A***) make a magic circle

<img class="rounded img-fluid post-img mx-auto d-block" src="/assets/images/IMG_0613-2-768x768.jpeg">

Round 1 – (***working into the magic circle***) ch3, 2tr, \*ch1, 3tr, repeat from \* twice more, ch1 then ss to top of ch3, fasten off.

<img class="rounded img-fluid post-img mx-auto d-block" src="/assets/images/IMG_0616-2-768x768.jpeg">

Round 2 – (***Using colour B***) join yarn in any ch1 space with a ss, ch3 (2tr, ch1, 3tr) into same space, (3tr, ch1, 3tr) into the 3 remaining ch1 spaces, ss to top of ch3.

<img class="rounded img-fluid post-img mx-auto d-block" src="/assets/images/IMG_0617-2-768x768.jpeg">

Round 3 – ss along to corner space, ch3 (2tr, ch1, 3tr) into same space, 3tr in next space (***change to colour C at the end of the 3rd tr***), (3tr, ch1, 3tr) in corner space (***change to colour B at the end of last tr***), 3tr in next space (***change to colour C at the end of the 3rd tr***), (3tr, ch1, 3tr) in corner space (***change back to colour B at the end of last tr***), 3tr in next space, (3tr, ch1, 3tr) in next corner space (***change to colour C at the end of last tr***), 3tr in next space, ss to top of ch3.

<img class="rounded img-fluid post-img mx-auto d-block" src="/assets/images/IMG_0619-1-768x768.jpeg">

**TIP – When crocheting round 4 try to crochet over as many of the ends from round 3 as possible, this will minimise the amount of ends to sew in.**

Round 4 – ch3, \*(3tr, ch1, 3tr) in corner space, 3tr in next 2 spaces, repeat from \* twice, (3tr, ch1, 3tr) in last corner space, 3tr in next space, 2tr in last space, ss to top of ch3.

<img class="rounded img-fluid post-img mx-auto d-block" src="/assets/images/Snapseed-1-768x768.jpeg">

Round 5 – ch3, 2tr in same space, \*(3tr, ch1, 3tr) in corner space, 3tr in next 3 spaces, repeat from \* twice, (3tr, ch1, 3tr) in last corner space, 3tr in next 2 spaces, ss to top of ch3, fasten off and sew in ends.

Block the square if necessary.
